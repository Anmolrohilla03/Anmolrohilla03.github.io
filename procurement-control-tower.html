Procurement Control Tower: Centralizing Workflows Across Teams

I designed a centralized control tower to unify procurement operations across internal teams — bringing together KPI dashboards, task tracking, training materials, and reporting tools into a single, scalable interface. This platform was tailored to support collaboration within the procurement department, simplify daily workflows, and increase leadership visibility across the procurement pipeline.

Procurement Control Tower - Landing Page

What Wasn't Working and What I Set Out to Solve

Challenge

  • 🧩 Scattered tools across teams (emails, spreadsheets, legacy dashboards)
  • ⏳ Manual delays in reporting & task tracking
  • 🔄 Miscommunication across departments
  • 👁️ Limited visibility for leadership
  • 📂 Training resources hard to access

Goal

  • 🧭 Centralize dashboards, tasks, training, and documentation
  • ⚡ Streamline workflows across procurement teams
  • 🧠 Improve decision-making through visibility
  • 📈 Build a scalable, intuitive, and efficient platform
  • 🔄 Enable faster onboarding and cross-functional adoption

Discover & Research

Insights gathered from procurement professionals guided every design decision.

I led the discovery, research, and ideation process to understand user needs and expectations. I conducted stakeholder interviews, distributed user surveys, and mapped emotional responses from the procurement team. These early insights revealed usability gaps, trust concerns, and workflow inefficiencies, which shaped the foundation of the design direction.

Which areas of the dashboard do you rely on most?
"The KPI card gives me a pulse check every morning. I wish I could rearrange or hide the ones not relevant to my role."
→ Add customizable KPI modules.
Do you find any part of the dashboard confusing or hard to locate?
"I wasn't sure at first where the RFP templates or pipeline analysis were. The links are there, but the titles could be more action-oriented."
→ Use contextual link labels like 'Open Template' and add icons.
How does the workflow/task area help your daily work?
"It's great to see tasks and systems linked — but a filter to see 'Overdue Only' would save me clicks."
→ Add task filters and sorting features.
Has this dashboard improved your team collaboration?
"The Yammer updates and reminders section are super helpful. It'd be even better if I could @tag someone in reminders."
→ Enable @mentions in notes or reminders.

High-Fidelity UI Design

Task Management & Reminders

A unified area for managing procurement workflows and deadlines. Users track key tasks, receive reminders, and stay on top of overdue reports from multiple systems.

Quick Access Resources

Procurement teams can instantly access frequently used documents, dashboards, and reports. This includes training material, Excel templates, RFP files, and more.

AI-Based Insights

Analytics-driven cards help users monitor KPIs and surface trends. This system is designed to evolve into AI-powered market recommendations and cost-saving suggestions.

Internal Communication

The built-in Yammer feed keeps procurement stakeholders updated with announcements, launch details, and contextual discussions — right inside the dashboard.

Onboarding Experience

01

To help users navigate the new Procurement Control Tower with ease, the onboarding feature launches an interactive walkthrough. It highlights key parts of the platform and explains their purpose step by step.

02

When users visit a section for the first time, contextual tooltips appear. For example, hovering over "Feedback" shows a pop-up explaining how to use it for reporting issues or suggesting improvements.

03

The guided tour includes a progress tracker, "Next" and "Skip" buttons, and highlights each section of the interface. This helps reduce confusion and improves user adoption for first-time users.

Integrated Support System

01

Users can access the Support Center directly from the dashboard navigation, without switching tools or platforms.

02

The support section includes searchable documentation, troubleshooting steps, and a feedback form to report issues.

03

Live chat and help ticket features are integrated, allowing procurement staff to get assistance without leaving their workflow.

Learnings & Reflections

This project pushed me to design for clarity within complexity — balancing stakeholder needs, system integrations, and real-time decision-making in a single dashboard. Collaborating with cross-functional teams taught me how to communicate design intent across non-design audiences and adapt quickly to feedback.

Designing for roles, not just features

Creating meaningful experiences meant tailoring content and visibility based on user roles, not just data hierarchy.

Feedback isn't just validation — it's evolution

Design critique sessions uncovered small friction points that had a big impact on user understanding.

A unified system improves more than tasks — it builds trust

Centralizing information helped reduce tool fatigue and increased stakeholder confidence in using the platform daily.

This project reminded me that good design doesn't just organize — it empowers.
