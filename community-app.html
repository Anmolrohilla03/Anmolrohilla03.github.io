Community Help

Community Help is an iOS application built for our community in aspects of safety and health. A lot of us live independently and with that comes safety. Community Help is an app which considers any medical or safety conditions which need immediate attention.

How the App Helps Your Community

Empowering communities through technology, making it easier to connect, help, and support each other.

Nearby Help

Discover people and organizations nearby offering food, shelter, education, and more.

Post a Request

Ask for help easily and share your needs with the local community.

Secure Chat

Communicate safely with volunteers and support providers in real-time.

Imagine working overtime at your workplace and returning home. Your first priority would be to reach home safely. In another case, let's say you are walking back home and you notice something suspicious around youof course you can run! But what if you could get help in a click? Lastly, what if you could save someone needing immediate medical assistance?

VOILA! WE FOUND OUR CUSTOMER

Solutions were designed using a user-centered process, validating needs at every step.

See It in Action

Experience how Community Help connects people and makes our neighborhoods safer and more supportive.

To build a solution that truly served the needs of the community, I followed a structured design process. The journey began with user interviews to uncover behavioral patterns, followed by distilling those insights into actionable outcomes. Below is an overview of the process that guided the development of this app.

User Interviews & What We Learned

How often do you work overtime?
✓ People often walk home late after work
How often do you take night strolls?
✓ Accident reporting helps users understand delays
Are you aware about neighborhood medical pharmacy and hospitals?
✓ New residents lack awareness of nearby support
Do you use Google Maps' accident reporting feature?
✓ Fear/confusion prevents incident reporting
Do you report suspicious behavior?
✓ Trust and clarity are needed for people to report
❝ "I usually don't know who to call unless it's an emergency." Real user insight
Name: Priya Sharma
Age: 29
Occupation: Software Engineer
Location: Suburban apartment, lives alone
Tech Comfort Level: High
Goals
  • Stay safe during late-night commutes from work
  • Get quick access to emergency support if needed
  • Be able to help others in her community without compromising privacy
Pain Points
  • Uncertainty about nearby hospitals or emergency contacts
  • Hesitation to report incidents due to unclear processes
  • Lack of centralized help/support app she can trust
Behaviors
  • Regularly walks home after late shifts
  • Uses Google Maps and Reddit for updates but not for help
  • Cares deeply about community safety but feels disconnected from local services
"I just want to know I'm not alone when walking home late. If something happens, there should be a way to get help fast."

Reporting a Crime

This screen group is designed to make reporting a crime fast, intuitive, and reassuring. The UI guides users step-by-step, reducing hesitation and ensuring all necessary details are captured. Clear calls-to-action and visual cues help users feel supported and safe throughout the process.

  • Step-by-step form flow for clarity and ease
  • Prominent visual cues and clear CTAs
  • Accessible color contrast and large tap targets
  • Supportive, confidence-building UI tone

Reporting an Incident

This group focuses on making incident reporting accessible and stress-free. The design uses clear steps, progress indicators, and helpful prompts to guide users, ensuring that even those unfamiliar with digital tools can complete the process confidently.

  • Guided, stepwise reporting for all users
  • Progress indicators and contextual help
  • Accessible navigation and error prevention
  • Friendly, non-intimidating interface

Reporting a Homeless

This group is tailored for reporting homeless individuals in need. The design emphasizes empathy and privacy, with clear options for anonymous reporting and resource suggestions, making it easy for users to offer help or alert local services.

  • Anonymous reporting and privacy-first design
  • Resource suggestions and local support info
  • Empathetic, non-judgmental language
  • Simple, accessible reporting steps
